Rice

Alternatives: Rice can be sold to rice processing industries or used to produce value-added products like rice bran oil, rice flour, and rice-based snacks. Farmers can also consider exporting to international markets where rice demand is high.

Contacts: Rice processing industries, international export firms, or local rice packaging businesses.

Wheat

Alternatives: Wheat can be processed into wheat flour, used to make wheat-based products like pasta and biscuits, or sold to food processing industries for bakery products. You can also store wheat in government granaries for better prices later.

Contacts: Flour mills, food processing companies, and bakeries.

Gram Dal

Alternatives: When prices are low, Gram Dal can be sold to snack manufacturers for making gram flour-based snacks like 'Besan' or gram flour sweets. Farmers can also produce and sell split gram or roasted gram for retail markets.

Contacts: Snack manufacturers, food processing units, and wholesale markets.

Mustard Oil (Packed)

Alternatives: Mustard seeds can be sold to oil processing units to produce oil and mustard cakes. These by-products are used in food industries or as animal feed. Mustard oil cakes can be used as organic fertilizers.

Contacts: Oil processing plants, fertilizer companies, and local food industries.

Potato

Alternatives: Potatoes can be sold to food industries to make potato chips, starch, or processed into frozen foods. Farmers can also store potatoes in cold storage to sell later at a higher price.

Contacts: Snack manufacturers, cold storage facilities, and starch processing plants.

Onion

Alternatives: Onions can be sold to dehydrating units to make onion powder or flakes, which are used in the food industry. Farmers can also export onions or store them in cold storage for selling during off-seasons.

Contacts: Dehydration units, exporters, and cold storage facilities.

Tomato

Alternatives: Tomatoes can be sold to food processing industries to make tomato puree, ketchup, sauces, and dried tomatoes. Farmers can also export tomatoes to international markets or store them for longer use.

Contacts: Tomato processing units, exporters, and local distributors.

Sugar

Alternatives: Sugar can be sold to food industries for producing sweets, confectioneries, and beverages. Farmers can also supply sugarcane to ethanol plants to produce biofuel.

Contacts: Confectionery industries, biofuel manufacturers, and beverage companies.

Milk

Alternatives: Milk can be processed into dairy products like cheese, butter, yogurt, and milk powder. You can also supply milk to large dairy processing companies for better returns.

Contacts: Dairy processing units, cheese and yogurt manufacturers.
